To build the model that is going to make the predictions I used data that is from Portugal that tracked the grade that the students got in their class along with 30 other variables; such as how much education their parents have, how long is their commute, do they have plans for higher education, how much they study, etc. After doing some research on the data showed me that Portugal uses a trimester system and that’s why each student has 3 grades for each course (G1, G2, G3). That is not the only thing interesting about their grades, they use a scale of 0 to 20, where a 10 is the lowest possible passing grade. But by far the most interesting bit is how they grade things, despite using a scale of 0 to 20, they do not make anything as a 20 because that would be a perfect score and there is always something to improve on. The data was titled ”Student Alcohol Consumption” which led me to assume that these were college age students, but a look at the students’ ages shows that it ranges from 15 to 22 and indicates that they are highschoolers.
